Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I have a grease trap or interceptor cleaned?

Cleaning frequency depends on the volume of FOG your kitchen produces and the size of your trap. Many jurisdictions say when the grease layer reaches 25 % of the unit or every 3-months at a minimum.

What happens if I don’t service my grease trap regularly?

If not maintained, grease traps can overflow, cause sewer backups, foul odours and attract fines from local authorities.

Can I clean or pump the trap myself?

While possible for small units, professional service is strongly recommended because of regulatory disposal, equipment needs and risk of improper cleaning.

What is the difference between a grease trap and a grease interceptor?

A grease trap is typically smaller, installed indoors or near sinks; an interceptor is larger, often underground or outdoors, handling higher volumes of grease.

Are there special disposal requirements for grease waste?

Yes. The collected grease and sludge must be handled and disposed of in compliance with local, state and federal environmental regulations.

What signs indicate my grease trap needs service soon?

Slow drains, foul odours, visible grease layer, backups or frequent disposal truck visits are all warning signs.

Leola, PA Insights: Population,
Zip Codes, Influence, and Service Areas

Leola is a census-designated place (CDP) in Lancaster County, Pennsylvania, United States. It includes the unincorporated communities of Leola, Leacock, and Bareville, and prior to 2010 was known as the Leacock-Leola-Bareville census-designated place. Originally named "Mechanicsburg", its present name is a portmanteau of "Leacock" and the "Glenola" train station that once served the town. As of the 2010 census, the population of the CDP was 7,214.
